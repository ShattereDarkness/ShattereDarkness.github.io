Storage

Introduction

There are 2 main categories of storage: HDDs, or Hard Disk Drives and SSDs, or Solid State Drives. In this day and age, many people still use HDDs. This is the main reason many people complain of slow loading times and a slow computer overall. HDDs are cheaper and offer more storage, but SSD, in 2020, is a necessity. The main differences are speed and reliablity

SSDs are significantly faster than HDDs. Even the slowest SSD is miles ahead of almost all HDDs. Just putting your operating system into an SSD gives faster boot times and app loading times. But this doesn't mean HDDs are obsolete. They are still very useful for mass storage, since SSDs are still more expensive. A small SSD combined with a large HDD is ideal.

Which type of SSD to buy?

If you look up SSDs on Amazon or some other website, then you may see many confusing and cryptic terms such as SATA, or NvMe. There are also different form factors - 2.5 inch ones and the thing stick like ones. Basically, there are 2 main types of SSDs - SATA and m.2 NVMe. SATA technology is older, and hence is cheaper, but m.2 is new and faster. SATA drives are usually 2.5 inch metallic drives, while m.2 drives are thin and go directly on the motherboard of the system.
